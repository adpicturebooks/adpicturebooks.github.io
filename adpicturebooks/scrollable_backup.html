Grandparents play an important role in the lives and upbringing of many children, and hence are often depicted in children’s literature. However, grandparents who are afflicted with Alzheimer's disease (AD) have been neglected, despite the global frequency of the disease. Diagnoses of AD in older people are becoming more common in recent years and more children may have personal experiences. Through the relationship between words and images in picturebooks, AD can be explored and discussed from various perspectives, giving children the opportunity to learn, question, and ultimately reflect on this sensitive topic. This website gives a brief overview of AD and presents picturebooks that deal with this disease in both an aesthetic and informative way.
